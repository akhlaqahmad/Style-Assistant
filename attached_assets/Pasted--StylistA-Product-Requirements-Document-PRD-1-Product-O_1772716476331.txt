# StylistA – Product Requirements Document (PRD)

## 1. Product Overview

**Product Name:** StylistA / Stylista AI

**Product Type:** Personal styling and wardrobe companion with a mobile‑first experience.

**Vision**

Enable users to understand their body proportions and tones in a positive, supportive way, digitise their wardrobe, generate daily outfits, plan travel capsules, and shop intentionally instead of impulsively.

The product guides users through body awareness, wardrobe organisation, outfit generation, and styling guidance while keeping the experience affirming and judgement‑free.

---

## 2. Target Users

### Primary Personas

**Busy Professional (25–45)**
Needs fast outfit decisions that look polished with minimal effort.

**Body‑Changing User**
Experiencing pregnancy, weight change, or life stage transitions and wants supportive guidance that adapts to their body.

**Style‑Curious User**
Interested in fashion but struggles to translate inspiration into practical outfits from their own wardrobe.

### Jobs To Be Done

Users want help to:

* Understand what clothing suits their body and why.
* Digitise and organise their wardrobe.
* Identify wardrobe gaps.
* Generate daily outfits based on mood, weather, and context.
* Pack efficiently for travel.
* Shop intentionally instead of accumulating unused clothing.
* Book professional stylists when needed.

---

## 3. Product Experience Overview

### Core Journey

1. **Onboarding and profiling**
2. **Body and tone analysis**
3. **Wardrobe digitisation**
4. **Daily outfit recommendations**
5. **Travel planner and packing**
6. **Feedback learning loop**
7. **Stylist marketplace and booking**
8. **Special event outfit requests**

The experience should feel supportive, personalised, and effortless.

---

## 4. MVP Scope

### Included in MVP

The MVP must deliver a fully navigable experience including:

* Complete onboarding flow
* Body proportion guidance screens
* Tone guidance screens
* Wardrobe scanning flow
* Wardrobe evaluation and tagging
* Style gaps discovery
* Suggested pieces view
* Daily outfit generator
* "Plan Tomorrow's Look" flow
* Travel planning and packing checklist
* Outfit feedback loop
* Stylist selection and booking flow
* Mock payment confirmation

User data should persist locally so the experience continues between sessions.

The system should record user actions such as onboarding completion, wardrobe additions, outfit generation, feedback submissions, trip creation, and stylist bookings.

### Excluded From MVP

The MVP does not require real device scanning, external services, or payment systems.

All such features should be represented with simulated behaviour or placeholder logic while maintaining a believable experience.

---

## 5. Detailed Functional Requirements

### 5.1 Onboarding and Profile Creation

Users create their styling profile through a guided sequence.

#### Information Collected

* Name (optional)
* Email (optional)
* Age range
* Gender identity
* Eye colour
* Hair colour
* Tanning behaviour
* Body features the user loves
* Body features the user prefers to downplay
* Colour preferences and dislikes
* Style familiarity
* Preferred styles or inspiration images
* Lifestyle habits
* Budget range

#### Tailored Experience Settings

Users can optionally enable:

* Weather‑aware outfit planning
* Night‑before outfit reminders
* Mood tracking
* Outfit feedback prompts
* Location‑based features

Users may also choose whether to generate a personal avatar.

All onboarding data should be saved progressively to avoid data loss if the user exits early.

---

### 5.2 Body Proportion Guidance

Users provide body measurements or estimated inputs.

These measurements are used internally to derive body proportions.

The system must never display body type labels.

Instead it provides positive guidance such as:

* Suggestions for flattering silhouettes
* Layering guidance
* Fabric and pattern suggestions

The system stores both the raw measurements (when available) and a derived body profile.

---

### 5.3 Tone Analysis

Tone classification is derived from:

* Eye colour
* Hair colour
* Tanning behaviour
* Colour preferences

The system determines whether the user aligns best with warm, cool, or neutral tones.

Users receive a colour palette along with simple guidance describing why those tones complement them.

---

### 5.4 Wardrobe Digitisation

Users scan clothing items into their digital wardrobe.

Each wardrobe item includes:

* Category
* Image
* Optional brand or label
* Optional notes

Each item receives a tag indicating whether it should be:

* Keep
* Review
* Donate

Users can mark items as favourites or hide them from outfit suggestions.

The wardrobe overview screen shows totals, category breakdown, and flagged gaps.

---

### 5.5 Style Gap Identification

The system analyses the wardrobe and lifestyle inputs to identify missing pieces.

Examples include:

* Missing layering pieces
* Lack of versatile shoes
* Absence of weather‑appropriate garments

The system presents a list of suggested pieces that could improve the wardrobe.

These suggestions include images and general descriptions.

---

### 5.6 Outfit Generation

The system generates daily outfit suggestions based on:

* Mood
* Context (work, weekend, event)
* Weather category
* Available wardrobe items
* Body profile
* Tone palette

Each generated outfit includes:

* Top
* Bottom or dress
* Shoes
* Optional accessories

Users can request additional options if they do not like the first suggestion.

---

### 5.7 Travel Planner

Users can plan outfits for trips.

Trip planning collects:

* Destination
* Dates
* Trip type
* Luggage type

The system creates:

* Day‑by‑day outfit plans
* A consolidated packing checklist

The planner should prioritise outfit reuse to minimise luggage.

---

### 5.8 Feedback Loop

Users provide feedback on daily outfits using a slider.

Score ranges correspond to sentiment categories ranging from dislike to love.

Depending on the score, users may be asked follow‑up questions about what worked or did not.

Feedback entries are stored and used to improve future outfit suggestions.

---

### 5.9 Stylist Marketplace and Booking

Users can browse professional stylists.

Each stylist profile includes:

* Specialty
* Pricing
* Availability

Users can request appointments and provide notes.

The system generates a booking summary and confirmation.

Payments are simulated in the MVP experience.

---

## 6. Non‑Functional Requirements

### Performance

Screens should load quickly and interactions should feel immediate.

### Privacy

Body data and images must be treated as sensitive personal information.

The system must request explicit permission before accessing device features.

### Accessibility

The experience should:

* Maintain readable contrast
* Provide labelled controls
* Use affirming language

### Resilience

The product should handle missing permissions gracefully and continue functioning without optional device capabilities.

---

## 7. Conceptual Data Model

The product maintains structured data representing:

* User profile
* Body profile
* Tone profile
* Wardrobe items
* Style gaps
* Suggested pieces
* Outfit plans
* Feedback entries
* Travel plans
* Stylist bookings

Each object should store timestamps and identifiers to enable future syncing and analytics.

---

## 8. External Integrations (Future Phases)

Future versions may integrate with:

* Weather services
* Retail partner catalogues
* Calendar access
* Payment gateways

These integrations are not required for the MVP but should be considered during system design.

---

## 9. Success Metrics

### Activation

* Percentage of users completing onboarding
* Percentage adding wardrobe items during first session

### Engagement

* Weekly active users
* Outfits generated per user
* Feedback submissions
* Travel plans created

### Commercial

* Stylist bookings
* Retail referral clicks

---

## 10. Product Roadmap

### MVP

Deliver a complete styling workflow with local persistence and simulated data sources.

### Phase 2

Introduce live integrations and a more advanced styling engine.

### Phase 3

Add advanced body scanning, avatar generation, and intelligent wardrobe analysis.
